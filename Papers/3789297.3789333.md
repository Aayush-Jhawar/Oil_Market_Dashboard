## Abstract
Crude oil prices represent one of the most critical macroeconomic
variables in the global economy, exerting profound impacts on
energy security, economic growth, and financial market stability.
However, their sharp fluctuations, nonlinear dynamics, volatility
clustering, and regime shifts undermine the predictive power of
traditional linear models. This study aims to enhance crude oil
price forecasting performance through deep learning techniques by
constructing and comparing several hybrid neural network mod-
els. We propose and evaluate four hybrid architectures: (1) CNNвҖ“
BiGRU, (2) CNNвҖ“Attention, (3) BiGRUвҖ“Attention, and (4) CNNвҖ“
BiGRUвҖ“Attention. Using daily West Texas Intermediate (WTI)
crude oil price data from 1990 to 2025, the CNNвҖ“BiGRUвҖ“Attention
model achieves the lowest prediction errors across multiple metrics:

## 1  Introduction
Crude oil is a pivotal global commodity whose price fluctuations
permeate the broader economy through channels such as trans-
portation costs, energy-weighted inflation indices, and corporate
procurement and inventory strategies. Accurate forecasting of oil
prices, even for single-step daily predictions, holds significant prac-
tical value. Marginal but consistent accuracy gains around price
turning points can substantially mitigate risks associated with sup-
ply disruptions and excessive hedging costs.
However, the inherent characteristics of oil marketsвҖ”such as fat-
tailed residuals, volatility clustering, asymmetric responses to sup-
ply and demand shocks, and frequent structural breaksвҖ”pose con-
siderable challenges to traditional linear models [1-2]. While classi-
cal time series approaches like ARIMA and exponential smoothing
capture low-frequency trends, they often fall short in modeling
the complex, nonlinear interactions among inventory levels, con-
venience yields, market expectations, and geopolitical risks [1].
Although regime-switching models introduce nonlinearity, their
reliance on strong assumptions regarding the number of states and
transition mechanisms limits their applicability during unconven-
tional events.
The rise of machine learning and deep learning offers promising
alternatives. Methods such as Support Vector Regression (SVR)
and tree-based models can handle nonlinear relationships but re-
quire extensive feature engineering and are prone to the curse of
dimensionality when incorporating multiple lags. In contrast, deep
sequential models inherently incorporate temporal inductive biases:
recurrent neural networks (RNNs) capture long-term dependencies,
convolutional neural networks (CNNs) extract local patterns, and at-
tention mechanisms adaptively weight informative time steps [3вҖ“6].
Recent empirical studies on crude oil markets confirm that deep
learning models, especially hybrid architectures, can substantially
improve short-term prediction accuracy compared with classical
econometric and shallow machine-learning models. Hu (2021)[12]
combines CEEMDAN decomposition, an LSTM-attention network

## 2  Literature Review
The forecasting of crude oil prices has evolved through several
distinct methodological phases, each addressing the limitations of
its predecessors while revealing new challenges.
Early research predominantly relied on linear time series models,
such as ARIMA and exponential smoothing, which perform well
in capturing stable trends and seasonal patterns under stationary
conditions. To account for the volatility clustering characteristic
of financial and commodity series, ARCH/GARCH-family mod-
els were introduced, shifting the focus to modeling conditional
variance. While foundational, these linear frameworks struggle
to accommodate the pronounced nonlinearities, structural breaks,
and complex regime-switching behavior inherent in oil markets
[1,2]. Although Markov-switching and threshold autoregressive
models introduced a degree of nonlinearity by allowing parameters
to change across different states, their effectiveness is often ham-
pered by the need for pre-specifying the number of regimes and
transition mechanisms, making them less adaptive to unforeseen
structural shocks.
The advent of machine learning (ML) offered a more flexible
toolkit for capturing nonlinear relationships without stringent para-
metric assumptions. Techniques such as Support Vector Regres-
sion (SVR), Random Forests, and gradient boosting (e.g., XGBoost)
demonstrated superior ability in learning complex patterns from
historical data. However, their application to time series forecasting
presents a critical drawback: a inherent lack oftemporal inductive
bias. These models require extensive feature engineering, typi-
cally involving the manual creation of lagged variables, which can
lead to the curse of dimensionality and increased model variance.
Consequently, their out-of-sample forecasting performance can de-
teriorate significantly when market dynamics shift, unless models
are frequently retrained.
Deep learning models represent a paradigm shift by intrinsi-
cally incorporating architectural preferences for sequential data.
Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs), and their more advanced vari-
ants Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) and Gated Recurrent Unit
(GRU), are explicitly designed to capture long-range temporal de-
pendencies, effectively mitigating the vanishing gradient problem
[3]. In parallel, one-dimensional Convolutional Neural Networks
(1D-CNNs) have been repurposed for time series analysis, proving
highly effective at extracting local temporal patterns and salient
features through shared filters and, when combined with dilated
convolutions, expanding the receptive field efficiently [5]. More
recently, the attention mechanism [4], a cornerstone of the Trans-
former architecture [6], has enabled dynamic, data-driven weight-
ing of relevant time steps, enhancing both model performance and
interpretability. A growing body of empirical evidence in energy
economics suggests that hybrid models, which combine these archi-
tectures (e.g., CNN-RNN or RNN-Attention), generally outperform
their standalone counterparts, particularly for short-term forecast-
ing tasks [7, 8].
The empirical properties of oil price returns, notably their fat-
tailed distributions and persistence, necessitate robust evaluation
frameworks. Conventional metrics like Mean Squared Error (MSE)
and Mean Absolute Error (MAE) can be disproportionately influ-
enced by a few extreme outliers. The QLIKE loss function [9] offers
a more robust alternative in such environments, especially when
true volatility is proxied. Furthermore, rigorous statistical testing is
paramount for validating forecast superiority. The Diebold-Mariano
(DM) test [11] assesses whether the difference in forecast accuracy
between two models is statistically significant, while the Model
Confidence Set (MCS) procedure [10] goes further by identifying a
subset of the best-performing models from a larger pool, controlling
for joint test size.
In summary, the literature progresses from linear models, which
are misspecified for oil price dynamics, to machine learning models,
which often ignore temporal structure, and finally to deep learning
models, which embed powerful inductive biases for sequence data.
A wide range of hybrid deep learning architecturesвҖ”such as CNNвҖ“
LSTM, CNNвҖ“BiLSTMвҖ“Attention and decompositionвҖ“ensemble
models coupled with GARCH-type volatility componentsвҖ”have
been proposed for crude oil price or volatility forecasting. However,

3.2.2  Ablation Models.For comparative analysis, we construct
three ablated variants:
1.CNN-Attention: This model removes the BiGRU layer, di-
rectly applying attention to the CNNвҖҷs output features. It

evaluates the necessity of dynamic temporal weighting.

applying attention directly to BiGRU hidden states. It serves
as a baseline to assess the value of local feature extraction.

## 6  Conclusion
This study set out to enhance the accuracy of short-term crude oil
price forecasting and to clarify the roles of different deep learn-
ing components by leveraging hybrid architectures. In pursuit
of this goal, we proposed, developed, and rigorously evaluated a
novel CNN-BiGRU-Attention model, systematically comparing its
performance against three strong baseline hybrids: CNN-BiGRU,
CNN-Attention, and BiGRU-Attention.
Our empirical analysis, conducted on a long-span dataset of WTI
daily prices from 1990 to 2025, yields several definitive conclusions.
First, the proposed CNN-BiGRU-Attention model demonstrates su-
perior predictive performance across all evaluation metrics, achiev-
ing the lowest RMSE (3.7897), MAE (2.1287), and QLIKE (34960.5),
and the highest R

(0.9569). This comprehensive success establishes
it as the most effective model for capturing the complex dynamics
of oil prices. Second, the ablation study inherent in our model com-
parison clearly delineates the distinct and critical contribution of
each component: the CNN module is vital for extracting local tem-
poral patterns and short-term volatility; the BiGRU layer is essential
for modeling long-term, bidirectional dependencies; and the atten-
tion mechanism provides a powerful dynamic weighting capability
that focuses the model on the most informative historical periods.
The synergy of these three components is the cornerstone of the
modelвҖҷs robustness, particularly during phases of high volatility
and structural change.
The statistical superiority of our proposed model is not merely
numerical but is firmly grounded in rigorous testing. The Diebold-
Mariano test consistently rejected the null hypothesis of equal pre-
dictive accuracy between our model and all baselines. Furthermore,
the Model Confidence Set procedure, at a 90% confidence level,
retained only the CNN-BiGRU-Attention model, offering strong
statistical evidence that it is the best-performing model within the
defined set.
From a practical standpoint, this research provides energy mar-
ket participantsвҖ”including policymakers, financial institutions, and
physical tradersвҖ”with a robust and advanced tool for price fore-
casting. The modelвҖҷs stability, especially around market turning
points, can directly contribute to improved risk management, more
effective hedging strategies, and more informed strategic planning
in the face of oil price uncertainty.
Looking forward, this work opens up several promising avenues
for future research. Firstly, the modelвҖҷs framework could be en-
riched by incorporating influential exogenous variables, such as
geopolitical risk indices, inventory levels, and key macroeconomic
indicators, to enhance its explanatory power. Secondly, moving
from point forecasts to probabilistic predictionsвҖ”for instance, by
employing Bayesian neural networks or quantile regression layersвҖ”
would provide valuable uncertainty quantification, which is critical
for risk-sensitive applications. Finally, exploring the applicability of
even more recent architectures, such as Transformers or Graph Neu-
ral Networks, which can model interconnections between different
commodity markets, presents a compelling direction for further
elevating the capabilities of energy price forecasting models.
In summary, this study confirms the significant potential of
hybrid deep learning models in commodity price forecasting. The
CNN-BiGRU-Attention architecture, in particular, offers a powerful,
robust, and insightful approach to navigating the complexities of
the global crude oil market.
